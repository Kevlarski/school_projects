Other Smithing Tools

The Right Tool for the Job

In addition to a style of tongs for every occasion, there are a number of other tools for all sorts of different applications.

  1. Hammers

    2. Rounding
    A rectuangular headed hammer. Generally one head is more rounded, and the other is a bit flatter.
    Cross-Peen
    One head is similar to the 'flat' side of the rounding hammer, the other is formed into a wedge shape for spreading material more effecitvely.
    Ball-Peen
    Generally a smaller weight than the other hammers one finds in a forge. One head is a smaller flat head, the other is fully rounded into a ball profile.
    Planishing
    A 'finishing' hammer. Both heads are fully flattened to smooth out work marks.

  2. Shapers

    3. Mandrel
    A cone used for sizing and shaping loops or curls
    Swedge
    A block of steel with various divots, wedges, and other forms to press material into.
    Bending Jig
    Often home-made, these are any variation of rods affixed so that a heated piece of metal can be bent to a specific curve.
    Fuller
    Essentially a dull chisel, the fuller pushes a trough into the material, spreading it out.

  3. Cutters

    4. Chisel
    Operates the same as a wood chisel, though often shaped differently.
    Hot Cut
    Can be handheld or an anvil tool. While a chisel can be used on colder material, a hot cut, as it's name implies, is intended to be used on hot material.
    Angle Grinder
    The only power tool on the list. Angle grinders are a staple in any sort of metalworking.
